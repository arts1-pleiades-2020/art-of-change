The Philippine Creative Industries Act

The Department of Trade and Industryâ€™s goal of becoming the leading creative economy in the ASEAN by 2030 seems to be absurd without legislation or a legitimized government support that will promote the development of creative industries. Hence, Paolo Mercado, along with Rep. De Venecia and the other instigators, proposed a bill that consolidates previous bills supporting creative industries not only to rationalize the need for government support but more importantly, to delineate our potential of becoming globally competitive in terms of arts. This bill is known as the Philippine Creative Industries Act of 2021.

The Philippine Creative Industries Act, in essence, seeks to promote creative industries as a priority economic sector to generate high-value jobs, contribute to GDP growth, increase international exports of Filipino creative products and services, and develop our intellectual property capital.

The creative industries are among the fastest growing sectors in our economy. However, with the ongoing pandemic and the implementation of strict precautionary measures, many workers have been displaced, and the arts community suffered greatly as a consequence. This consolidated bill will not only help the affected workers in the creative industries to survive this crisis but also support and sustain this sector looking ahead on the road to national recovery.

The key elements of the bill are as follows:

  • Scope of the Act

    It clearly defines the scope of Philippine creative industries to include nine domains: (1) audio and audiovisual media; (2) digital interactive media; (3) books, publishing, and printed media; (4) creative services; (5) design; (6) visual arts; (7) performing arts; (8) traditional cultural expressions; (9) and cultural sites.
